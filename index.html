Leonidas,
King of Sparta

Early Life:
  1. Leonidas was born in about 540 B.C
  2. He was the son of the Spartan King Anaxandrides.
  3. Harsh early life due to Spartan children being brought up under extreme discipline
  4. Trained mentally and physically on how to fight from a young age
  5. Trained to be a hoplite warrior (frontal fighters who used the phalanx formation)
Major Accomplishments:
  1. King of Sparta from 490-480 B.C.
  2. Famous for the Battle of Thermopylae
  3. Led an army of 6-7k Greeks and around 300 Spartans to prevent the Persians from getting through the coastal pass of Thermopylae
  4. Defended the pass for 2 days against the far larger Persian army
  5. Eventually was surrounded but the Persians but didnâ€™t flee and he died fighting them off
Societal Contributions:
  1. His sacrifice allowed the Greeks to regroup and prepare and ultimately win in the Battle of Salamis
  2. Became an enduring tale of Greek heroism
  3. His story is passed down as the epitome of bravery despite the odds
  4. Led to societies positive view of Spartans and legends being created about how the never give up
Fun Facts:
  1. The stories say Leonidas is a descendant of Heracles (Greek name for Hercules)
  2. Sparta used iron rods as currency not coins
  3. Sparta was not only famous for its fighting, but also music and poetry
  4. Leonidas's name either meant son of a lion or like a lion
  5. The first female Olympian was Spartan
